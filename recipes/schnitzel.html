Schnitzel originates out of Germany and Austria.

This cuisine rose to fame through the Austro-Hungarian Empire in the late 19th century

Schnitzel globally expanded in the 20th century as Austrian and German immigrants brought the recipe abroad.

Ingredients

  • One and 1/2 pounds of veal cutlets
  • 1/2 cup all purpose flowers
  • Two large eggs
  • Three tablespoons grated parmesan cheese
  • Two tablespoons of milk
  • One teaspoon of minced parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • One pinch ground nutmeg
  • One cup dry bread crumbs
  • Six tablespoons butter
  • Four slices lemon

Steps for cooking

  1. Place veal cutlets between 2 sheets of heavy plastic on a solid, level surface.
  2. Firmly pound cutlets with the smooth side of a meat mallet to a 1/4-inch thickness.
  3. Dip cutlets in flour to coat; shake off excess.
  4. Beat eggs, Parmesan cheese, milk, parsley, salt, pepper, and nutmeg together in a shallow bowl until combined.
  5. Place bread crumbs on a plate.
  6. Dip each cutlet into the egg mixture, then press in bread crumbs to coat.
  7. Place coated cutlets on a plate and refrigerate for 1 hour to overnight.
  8. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
  9. Cook breaded cutlets in butter until browned, about 3 minutes per side.
  10. Transfer cutlets to a serving platter and pour pan juices over them.
  11. Garnish with lemon slices.
  12. Enjoy!
